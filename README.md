# Cross-Market-Index-Comparison
# Macroeconomic Influences on Equity Indices: JPY225 vs US30

## Overview

This repository contains a quantitative macro-financial research project analyzing the performance of Japan’s Nikkei 225 (JPY225) and the Dow Jones Industrial Average (US30) over the period July 2023 to December 2025.

The central output of this project is a structured analytical report that investigates how macroeconomic divergence, currency fluctuations, sector composition, and geopolitical shocks jointly influence cross-market equity performance.

The work adopts a research-driven framework commonly used in macroeconomic and financial markets analysis, combining time-series interpretation, correlation structure analysis, event-driven evaluation, and probabilistic forecasting.

## Analytical Scope

The study is structured around seven key analytical dimensions:

### 1. Market Performance Evolution
A comparative analysis of the long-term performance trajectories of the Nikkei 225 and Dow Jones Industrial Average under shifting global macroeconomic conditions.

### 2. Currency and Macro Divergence Effects
An examination of how USD/JPY exchange rate dynamics influence relative equity performance, particularly in the context of divergent monetary policy between the Bank of Japan and the Federal Reserve.

### 3. Correlation Structure Analysis
A statistical assessment of cross-market relationships, evaluating the degree of co-movement between:
- Nikkei 225
- Dow Jones Industrial Average
- USD/JPY exchange rate

### 4. Sectoral Contribution Analysis
A decomposition of index performance into underlying sector drivers, identifying which industries contributed most significantly to observed returns across both markets.

### 5. Market Sentiment and Volatility Regimes
An analysis of volatility behavior and sentiment shifts using market-based volatility proxies, highlighting periods of stress, recovery, and stabilization.

### 6. Geopolitical and Tariff Shock Analysis
An event-driven evaluation of market reactions to trade policy shocks and geopolitical developments, focusing on short-term disruptions and recovery dynamics.

### 7. Forward-Looking Simulation
A probabilistic forecast of index performance using Monte Carlo simulation to estimate potential future distribution paths over a 6-month horizon.

## Key Findings

- Global equity markets exhibit strong co-movement under shared macroeconomic regimes, but structural divergence emerges under currency and policy asymmetry.
- USD/JPY exchange rate movements are a critical determinant of relative performance between Japanese and U.S. equities.
- The Dow Jones demonstrates stronger momentum persistence, while the Nikkei 225 exhibits higher sensitivity to external currency and capital flow conditions.
- Sector composition plays a significant role in amplifying or dampening macroeconomic effects across indices.
- Geopolitical shocks produce synchronized short-term drawdowns, followed by regionally differentiated recovery patterns.
- Forward-looking simulations indicate positive expected returns with asymmetric risk distributions across both indices.

## Notes on Methodology

This project is intended as a macro-financial research exercise. The analysis is structured to reflect real-world financial research workflows, including:

- Cross-market comparative equity analysis
- Macro regime interpretation
- Currency exposure evaluation
- Event-based market reaction assessment
- Probabilistic forecasting of financial time series

## Interpretation

The results highlight that equity index performance cannot be interpreted solely through domestic fundamentals. Instead, outcomes are shaped by an interaction between global liquidity conditions, monetary policy divergence, sectoral composition, and currency translation effects.

This reinforces the importance of multi-factor macroeconomic frameworks in cross-economy equity analysis.
